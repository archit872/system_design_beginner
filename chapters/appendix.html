Appendix — Pitfalls, Review & Progression

A grab-bag of practical aids: the top 10 pitfalls (with fixes), a review drill pack, and a 4–6 week progression plan—so you keep improving after finishing the eight chapters.

A.1 Pitfalls Checklist (with fixes)

  1. Skipping requirements: State functional + non-functional targets first (p95, RPS, storage/day).
  2. Capacity hand-waving: Use the quick formula replicas ≈ ceil(peak_RPS / (RPS_per_replica × headroom)).
  3. Misplaced caches: Start at edge → app → DB. Add TTL + jitter and explicit busting.
  4. Premature microservices: Monolith + cache until metrics prove the need.
  5. Undefined consistency: Label strong vs. eventual; add read-your-writes where needed.
  6. No fault plan: Timeouts, backoff, circuit breakers, bulkheads, graceful degradation.
  7. Ignoring data model: Design from top queries to keys/indexes; avoid full scans.
  8. Weak observability: Define SLIs/SLOs, add request IDs, dashboards, alerts with runbooks.
  9. Security gaps: TLS everywhere; token auth; rate limits; sensitive data out of logs.
  10. Unowned risks: Name 3 risks + mitigations in every design (e.g., hot keys, abuse, failover).

A.2 Review Drills

Vocabulary Speed Round

Define each term in one sentence and give a concrete example: latency, throughput, availability, durability, consistency, idempotency, TTL, SLI/SLO, backpressure, circuit breaker.

Explain Like I’m New

Pick one topic (e.g., caching) and explain it to a junior dev using an analogy, a diagram, and a 3-step practice task.

Compare & Contrast

  • REST vs. gRPC vs. WebSockets
  • SQL vs. Document vs. Wide-column
  • Scale up vs. scale out
  • Active–active vs. active–passive

Trade-off Lab

Write 4–6 trade-offs for your capstone (e.g., URL shortener): what you gained, what you gave up, why acceptable.
